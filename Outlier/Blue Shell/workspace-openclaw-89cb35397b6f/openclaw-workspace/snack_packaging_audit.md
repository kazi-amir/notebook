### 2b. FDA Guidance Summary

**FDA Food Labeling Guide (06):**
- The 8 major allergens (milk, egg, fish, crustacean shellfish, tree nuts, peanuts, wheat, soybeans) must be declared parenthetically in the ingredient list or via a "Contains" statement.
- Net weight must appear in the bottom 30% of the principal display panel in both metric and U.S. customary units.
- This guide (Jan 2013) predates the gluten-free labeling rule (21 CFR 101.91, Aug 2013) and does not cover it.

**FDA Food Allergen Guidance (07):**
- Mandatory "Contains" statements cover allergens intentionally added as ingredients.
- Cross-contact allergens (shared equipment) are **not** included in mandatory declarations; "may contain" advisories are voluntary and separate.
- A "Contains" statement, if used, must list **all** major allergens present — not just a subset.
- Gluten-free labeling is governed separately under 21 CFR 101.91 (not covered in this document).

## 5. Selection Reasoning

**Hard filters applied in order:**

1. **In stock** — all four pass.
2. **No wheat declared** — eliminates **D (Chex Mix)** (wheat in "Contains").
3. **Package size matches CSV** — eliminates **A (SkinnyPop)** (1 oz photo ≠ 4.4 oz CSV).
4. **At least one must clearly say gluten-free** — among remaining B and C, **B (KIND)** satisfies this ("GLUTEN FREE" on front).
5. **≤ $18 total** — B ($8.49) + C ($5.99) = **$14.48** ✅

**Final two selections: B (KIND) + C (Nature Valley)**

- **B** is the most trail-mix-similar option (nut-based, contains almonds), carries a gluten-free label, and its package matches the CSV.
- **C** has a matching package size, no wheat declared, a competitive price, and complements B as a different snack type (granola bar vs. nut bar). However, note that C does **not** carry a gluten-free label — if strict gluten-free certification is required for **both** picks, C would be a risk.
