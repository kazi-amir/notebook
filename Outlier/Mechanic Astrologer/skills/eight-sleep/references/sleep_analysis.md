# Sleep Data Analysis Guide

Use this guide to interpret Eight Sleep Pod sleep metrics and provide science-backed insights.

## 5-Step Analysis Framework

1. **Status**: Current state of each metric (good/fair/poor)
2. **Trends**: Direction over time (improving/stable/declining)
3. **Patterns**: Day-of-week effects, temperature correlations, consistency
4. **Insights**: Actionable takeaways based on the data
5. **Flags**: Red flags that suggest medical consultation

Always include a disclaimer: "This analysis is for informational purposes only and is not medical advice."

---

## Sleep Fitness Score

The sleep fitness score (0-100) is the primary metric. It combines duration, quality, and stage composition.

| Range | Rating | Description |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| 85-100 | Excellent | Optimal sleep across all dimensions |
| 70-84 | Good | Meeting most sleep goals |
| 55-69 | Fair | Room for improvement in duration or quality |
| 40-54 | Poor | Significant sleep issues detected |
| <40 | Very Poor | Severely impacted sleep, investigate causes |

### What affects the score
- **Duration**: Sleeping within 30 minutes of your target hours
- **Stage balance**: Healthy proportions of deep, REM, light, awake
- **Consistency**: Regular bedtime and wake time
- **Restfulness**: Minimal awake time during the night

## Sleep Stages

### Deep Sleep (Slow Wave Sleep)
Critical for physical recovery, immune function, and memory consolidation.

| Age | Healthy Range | Concern Threshold |
|-----|--------------|-------------------|
| 20-29 | 15-25% | <13% |
| 30-39 | 13-23% | <11% |
| 40-49 | 10-20% | <8% |
| 50-59 | 8-15% | <5% |
| 60+ | 5-12% | <3% |

**Factors that increase deep sleep:** Exercise (4+ hours before bed), cooler bed temperature, consistent sleep schedule, avoiding alcohol.

**Factors that decrease deep sleep:** Alcohol, caffeine after 2pm, stress, irregular schedule, aging.

### REM Sleep
Essential for emotional processing, creativity, and memory consolidation.

| Metric | Normal | Watch |
|--------|--------|-------|
| REM % | 20-25% | <15% or >30% |
| REM timing | More in second half of night | Absent in early cycles |

**Factors that affect REM:** Alcohol suppresses REM, antidepressants can alter REM, sleep deprivation causes REM rebound.

### Light Sleep
Normal transition stage. Not inherently bad -- serves important functions.

| Metric | Normal | Watch |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Light % | 45-55% | >65% (may indicate fragmented sleep) |

### Awake Time
Time spent awake during the sleep period.

| Metric | Normal | Concern |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Awake % | 2-8% | >15% suggests sleep maintenance issues |
| Wake count | 0-2 brief | >5 full awakenings |

## Heart Rate Variability (HRV)

HRV during sleep reflects recovery and autonomic nervous system balance. Higher values generally indicate better recovery.

### Normal Ranges by Age (during sleep)

| Age | Low | Average | High |
|-----|-----|---------|------|
| 20-29 | <40 | 40-80 | >80 |
| 30-39 | <35 | 35-70 | >70 |
| 40-49 | <25 | 25-55 | >55 |
| 50-59 | <20 | 20-45 | >45 |
| 60+ | <15 | 15-35 | >35 |

### Interpreting HRV Trends
- **Rising trend**: Improving recovery, good training adaptation
- **Stable**: Consistent recovery (good if in normal range)
- **Declining**: Possible overtraining, illness, stress, poor sleep
- **Sudden drop** (>20% below baseline): Illness onset, excessive training, major stressor

## Resting Heart Rate (During Sleep)

Sleep heart rate is typically 10-20% lower than daytime resting HR.

| Category | Sleep HR Range |
|----------|---------------|
| Athlete | 38-50 bpm |
| Very Fit | 45-55 bpm |
| Fit | 50-60 bpm |
| Average | 55-70 bpm |
| Above Average | 65-80 bpm |

### Trends
- **Lower over weeks**: Improving cardiovascular fitness
- **Sudden elevation** (>5 bpm above normal): Possible illness, dehydration, overtraining

---

## Respiratory Rate

Normal sleep respiratory rate: 12-20 breaths per minute.

| Rate | Interpretation |
|------|---------------|
| 12-16 | Normal |
| 16-20 | Slightly elevated (may indicate stress or illness) |
| >20 | Elevated -- monitor for illness, consult doctor if persistent |
| <10 | Unusually low -- verify sensor placement |

## Temperature Optimization

The Eight Sleep Pod controls bed temperature to optimize sleep stages.

### General Guidelines
- **Bedtime**: Cool the bed (-10 to -30) to help initiate sleep. Core body temperature needs to drop 1-2°F to fall asleep.
- **Deep Sleep**: Cooler temperatures (-20 to -50) promote deeper sleep in the first half of the night.
- **REM Sleep**: Slightly warmer than deep sleep phase, as REM requires some thermoregulation.
- **Wake-Up**: Gradual warming (+10 to +30) in the last 30-60 minutes can improve natural waking.

### Temperature and Stage Correlations
- **Lower temperatures** correlate with more deep sleep
- **Stable temperatures** correlate with fewer awakenings
- **Warming before alarm** correlates with more natural, refreshed waking

## Sleep Consistency

Regular sleep timing is one of the strongest predictors of sleep quality.

### Metrics to Track
- **Bedtime variance**: Ideally <30 minutes day-to-day
- **Wake time variance**: Ideally <30 minutes day-to-day
- **Weekend shift**: Sleeping >1 hour later on weekends indicates social jet lag

### Social Jet Lag Warning Signs
- Weekend bedtime >1.5 hours later than weekday
- Monday morning grogginess
- Declining sleep scores early in the week

---

## Pattern Detection

### Look For
1. **Day-of-week patterns**: Lower scores Monday (weekend shift), higher Friday (accumulated sleep debt)
2. **Temperature correlations**: Compare nights with different temp settings to sleep scores
3. **Exercise effect**: Better sleep 1-2 days after moderate exercise
4. **Alcohol effect**: Higher HR, lower HRV, suppressed deep/REM on nights with alcohol
5. **Stress patterns**: Declining HRV with increased awake time

### Red Flags (Recommend Medical Consultation)
- Persistent sleep fitness scores <50 for >2 weeks
- Average awake time >20% consistently
- Respiratory rate consistently >20
- HRV consistently declining over 2+ weeks
- Sleep duration consistently <5 hours despite adequate opportunity

## Actionable Recommendations

### For Low Sleep Fitness Scores
1. Ensure consistent bedtime (within 30 min window)
2. Optimize bed temperature (try cooler settings)
3. Limit screen time 1 hour before bed
4. Avoid caffeine after 2pm, alcohol within 3 hours of bedtime

### For Low Deep Sleep
1. Try cooler bed temperature (-30 to -50)
2. Exercise during the day (not within 3 hours of bedtime)
3. Maintain consistent sleep schedule
4. Consider magnesium supplementation (consult doctor)

### For Low HRV
1. Reduce training intensity temporarily
2. Prioritize sleep duration (7-9 hours)
3. Manage stress (meditation, breathing exercises)
4. Ensure adequate hydration

### For High Awake Time
1. Check bed temperature comfort
2. Reduce evening fluid intake
3. Address potential environmental disruptions (noise, light)
4. Consider relaxation techniques before bed
