# Health Data Analysis Guide

Use this guide to interpret Whoop health metrics and provide science-backed insights.

## 5-Step Analysis Framework

1. **Status**: Current state of each metric (good/fair/poor)
2. **Trends**: Direction over time (improving/stable/declining)
3. **Patterns**: Day-of-week effects, correlations between metrics
4. **Insights**: Actionable takeaways based on the data
5. **Flags**: Red flags that suggest medical consultation

Always include a disclaimer: "This analysis is for informational purposes only and is not medical advice."

---

## Heart Rate Variability (HRV)

HRV (measured as RMSSD in milliseconds) reflects autonomic nervous system balance and recovery capacity.

### Normal Ranges by Age

| Age | Low | Average | High |
|-----|-----|---------|------|
| 20-29 | <40 | 40-80 | >80 |
| 30-39 | <35 | 35-70 | >70 |
| 40-49 | <25 | 25-55 | >55 |
| 50-59 | <20 | 20-45 | >45 |
| 60+ | <15 | 15-35 | >35 |

### Interpretation

- **Rising trend**: Improved fitness, better recovery, reduced stress
- **Declining trend**: Overtraining, illness onset, elevated stress, poor sleep
- **High variability day-to-day**: Normal; focus on 7-day rolling average
- **Sudden drop (>30%)**: Potential illness, significant stressor, or overreaching

### Key Insight
Individual baseline matters more than population norms. Compare to the user's own 30-day average.

## Resting Heart Rate (RHR)

### Normal Ranges

| Fitness Level | RHR (bpm) |
|---------------|-----------|
| Athlete | 40-55 |
| Excellent | 55-60 |
| Good | 60-65 |
| Average | 65-75 |
| Below average | 75-85 |
| Poor | >85 |

### Interpretation

- **Decreasing trend**: Improved cardiovascular fitness
- **Increasing trend**: Overtraining, dehydration, stress, illness
- **Elevated 2-3 bpm above baseline**: May indicate early illness or incomplete recovery
- **RHR and HRV typically move inversely**: RHR up + HRV down = reduced recovery

## Recovery Score (0-100%)

### Zones

| Zone | Score | Meaning | Recommendation |
|------|-------|---------|----------------|
| Green | 67-100% | Well recovered | Safe for high strain |
| Yellow | 34-66% | Partially recovered | Moderate activity recommended |
| Red | 0-33% | Not recovered | Light activity or rest |

### Factors Affecting Recovery

- Sleep quality and duration (strongest factor)
- HRV and RHR (physiological readiness)
- Previous day's strain
- Alcohol consumption
- Stress levels
- Illness or infection

### Pattern Detection

- **Consecutive red days (3+)**: Possible overtraining or illness
- **Recovery doesn't match sleep**: Look at strain, stress, or lifestyle factors
- **Weekend vs weekday patterns**: Common to see higher recovery on weekends

## Sleep Metrics

### Sleep Duration Needs

| Age | Recommended |
|-----|-------------|
| 18-25 | 7-9 hours |
| 26-64 | 7-9 hours |
| 65+ | 7-8 hours |

### Sleep Stage Distribution (Healthy Adults)

| Stage | % of Total Sleep | Function |
|-------|-----------------|----------|
| Light (N1+N2) | 50-60% | Memory consolidation, body maintenance |
| Deep/SWS (N3) | 15-25% | Physical recovery, growth hormone, immune function |
| REM | 20-25% | Emotional processing, memory, learning |

### Sleep Performance

- **>85%**: Excellent - meeting sleep needs
- **70-85%**: Good - minor sleep debt
- **<70%**: Poor - accumulating sleep debt

### Sleep Efficiency

- **>90%**: Excellent - minimal time awake in bed
- **80-90%**: Good
- **<80%**: May indicate sleep disorders or poor sleep hygiene

### Sleep Consistency

- **>80%**: Excellent - regular sleep/wake times
- **60-80%**: Fair - some variability
- **<60%**: Poor - irregular schedule, circadian disruption

### Key Insights

- Deep sleep is front-loaded (first half of night); alcohol suppresses it
- REM increases in second half of night; early wake-ups cut REM
- Sleep debt accumulates but recovery is not 1:1 (you can't fully "catch up")
- Respiratory rate: normal 12-20 breaths/min; sustained elevation may indicate illness

## Day Strain (0-21)

### Strain Scale

| Range | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 0-3 | Rest | Minimal physical activity |
| 4-7 | Low | Light activity (walking, easy yoga) |
| 8-13 | Moderate | Regular exercise, active day |
| 14-17 | High | Intense training session |
| 18-21 | Overreaching | Extreme exertion, competition level |

### Strain-Recovery Balance

- **Strain > Recovery capacity**: Accumulated fatigue, injury risk
- **Chronic high strain + declining HRV**: Overtraining syndrome warning
- **Average daily strain 10-14**: Typical for active individuals
- **Average daily strain >16**: Unsustainable without adequate recovery

### Workout Strain vs Day Strain

Day strain includes all activity (walking, work, exercise). Workout strain is a subset. The gap between them indicates non-exercise activity level.

## SpO2 (Blood Oxygen Saturation)

### Normal Ranges

| Level | SpO2 % | Interpretation |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| Normal | 95-100% | Healthy oxygen saturation |
| Mild concern | 93-95% | May indicate respiratory issue or altitude |
| Seek attention | <93% | Potentially significant; consult a doctor |

### Context

- Altitude affects SpO2 (lower at elevation)
- Sleep apnea can cause overnight drops
- Single low reading may be sensor error; look for patterns

## Skin Temperature

### Normal Range

- Typical: 35.5-37.0 C
- Individual baseline varies; track deviations from personal norm

### Interpretation

- **Elevated 0.5+ C above baseline**: May indicate illness onset, hormonal changes, or overtraining
- **Consistently elevated**: Could indicate chronic inflammation
- **Menstrual cycle**: Temperature rises ~0.3-0.5 C after ovulation (luteal phase)

## Pattern Detection Checklist

When analyzing data, look for:

1. **Overtraining signals**: Declining HRV + rising RHR + poor recovery over 5+ days
2. **Sleep debt accumulation**: <7h average over 5+ days
3. **Illness onset**: Elevated RHR, elevated skin temp, declining HRV, SpO2 drop
4. **Day-of-week effects**: Lower recovery on Mondays (weekend behavior), higher strain on certain days
5. **Strain-recovery mismatch**: High strain days followed by insufficient recovery
6. **Sleep architecture issues**: Low deep sleep (<15%), low REM (<15%)
7. **Consistency problems**: High sleep time variability (>1h) night to night

---

## Red Flags (Suggest Medical Consultation)

- SpO2 consistently below 94%
- RHR increase >10 bpm above baseline for 3+ days
- Skin temperature elevated >1 C above baseline for 3+ days
- Respiratory rate consistently >20 breaths/min
- Recovery score in red zone for 5+ consecutive days with adequate sleep
- Sudden HRV drop >50% from baseline without obvious cause

---

## Providing Recommendations

When giving advice based on data:

1. **Be specific**: "Your HRV has declined 15% over the past week" not "Your HRV is low"
2. **Compare to their baseline**: Use their 30-day average as reference
3. **Suggest actionable steps**: "Consider reducing training intensity for 2-3 days"
4. **Acknowledge limitations**: Wearable data is estimates, not clinical measurements
5. **Always disclaim**: This is not medical advice; consult a healthcare provider for health concerns
