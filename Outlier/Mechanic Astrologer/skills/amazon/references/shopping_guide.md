## Product Comparison Tips

When comparing products, consider these factors:

### Price vs Value
- Compare price per unit for consumables
- Factor in Prime eligibility for free shipping value
- Check if a higher-priced item has significantly better ratings

### Ratings & Reviews
- Products are rated 4.0–4.9 in this catalog
- Higher review counts indicate more data points (more reliable rating)
- A 4.5 rating with 50,000 reviews is generally more trustworthy than 4.9 with 100 reviews

### Brand Considerations
- Established brands (Apple, Samsung, Sony) typically offer better warranty support
- Store brands / Amazon Basics can offer significant savings for basics
- Niche brands may excel in their specialty category

## Spending Analysis Guidance

### Monthly Trends
- Compare 3+ months to identify patterns
- Seasonal spikes (Nov–Dec holidays, back-to-school in Aug–Sep) are normal
- Sudden increases may indicate one-time large purchases vs habit changes

### Category Balance
- A diverse category spread suggests the user uses Amazon as a general store
- Heavy concentration in one category may indicate a hobby or professional need
- Compare category spending to identify where the user could save

### Order Frequency
- **High frequency** (10+ orders/month): Power user, likely benefits from Prime
- **Medium** (4–9/month): Regular shopper
- **Low** (1–3/month): Selective purchaser, may do more research per purchase

### Cancellation Patterns
- Occasional cancellations are normal (found a better deal, changed mind)
- High cancellation rate (>15%) may suggest impulse buying followed by regret
- Check if cancellations cluster in specific categories

## Smart Shopping Tips

1. **Use search filters**: Narrow by category, brand, price range, and rating to find the best match faster
2. **Check favorites**: Frequently purchased items may be available cheaper in bulk
3. **Track spending by month**: Set a budget and monitor against monthly totals
4. **Compare brands**: Use the brands command to see what's available in a category before committing
5. **Review order history**: Past purchases inform future decisions — check what worked and what didn't
